The Next Generation of Computing

Where things are headed, and my plans for 2022 onwards.

Tablets are the new laptops

With the advent of GitHub Codespaces, VSCode, StackBlitz, and others, its very clear where the modern development environment is moving. to the cloud!

Imagine a scenario where companies no longer need to supply employees with ultra-super-powerful-mega computing devices. All expensive processing is handled in a private cloud and streamed back to the accessing thin-client.

What would cost a company roughly ~2k for a new Macbook Pro, can now achieved with a low-end laptop or tablet device. Repairing and maintaining company equipment is reduced by a large factor, and can be easily replaced without breaking the bank. This is especially valuable when it comes to small business/startups.

Environment issues no-more

As GitHub had mentioned in their blog post about GitHub Codespaces, employees were able to avoid weird and tedious environment issues utilizing Codespaces as the interface to the GitHub source itself.

Onboarding processes in-turn will be much easier, avoiding large (and often outdated) README.md files explaining how to setup local environments.

Access anywhere, from anything*that has internet access

Forgetting your device at home or at a friend/relatives/etc place will be a thing of the past. Any internet capable device that has access to a (relatively) modern web browser will allow you to continue where you left off with no extra overhead.

My plans for 2022 Onwards

My plan for the remainder of 2021 is to continue to refine and explore utilizing my tablet to move more of my workflow to different clouds (private, and public). In addition, I will create, and maintain a SaaS product solely using my tablet device (to the best of my abilities, afaik its totally doable).

This whole post was written with GitHub Codespaces on an iPad air.
